And I Study Mechanical Engineering

Engineering

Everything we interact with in our daily lives was made by someone: the mattress you woke up on, the frying pan you cooked your eggs in, the screen you're reading this on. Engineers are the "makers" behind each of these items, and they hold tremendous power in directing how we interact with the world. I have always wanted to help people, and engineering enables me to make people's lives easier through changing the way they interact with the world. As a child I always liked building things, but was ushered toward more traditional toys and activities, making dinner with my mom while my brother helped my dad around the house. It wasn't until college that I realized I could pursue and be successful in such a powerful field.

Family and Home

Family, to me, consists of the people who contribute to your personal growth and support you on your path through life. The biggest contributor to my development thus far has been my older brother, Calvin. From teaching me about healthy relationship habits to encouraging me to build things, to sending me homemade toffee in the mail when he can't be there in person to give me a hug, Calvin has always had my back. Our parents consider our relationship to be their crowning achievement. After our parents split, they made Calvin and me their top priority, working through difficult situations to ensure we had some semblance of normalcy; I admire their grace. "Family" started to look like three-person dinners, but it felt the same. Home, too, took on a different meaning as our childhood home was sold, my parents bounced around different living arrangements, and Calvin and I started to explore our role in the world. I spent a year in Australia as an exchange student during high school, and my sense of family and home grew yet again.

Entertainment

This video, Ze Frank's "Notes on Friendship", shaped me in multiple ways: for one, I found this video as a homesick fifteen-year-old halfway across the world from everyone I had previously considered friends, and the line spoke to me: "Maybe the greatest gift you give me is allowing me to stop struggling to find my place. We're friends; that is the place. It's an extension of what I call home." It also was a soft introduction to spoken word, which I would fully discover years later in a cafe basement in Edinburgh. Media has the power to educate, to influence, to ignite, and to comfort, and we are constantly engaging with it, consciously and subconsciously. We are consumers, always, and mindfulness of media consumption goes a long way.

Community

I have always been a joiner, rapid-iterating versions of my life with different extracurriculars and their accompanying microcultures. I have been a theatre kid, an orchadork, a rower, a Science Olympian, a refugee mentor, a cubicle-dweller, but I find my community with makers, sailors, and helpers. Starting a club, sailing competitively, and working at a soup kitchen have shown me that each person is influential in the communities they participate in and has the ability to change the culture, for better or worse. That said, I find peace and choose to exist in groups that are kind and forward-thinking.

School

Learning is a lifelong endeavor, and I believe that the only failures you experience are the ones you don't learn from. I missed out on AP classes to go to Australia, but I learned the scope of my resiliency. I got zero usable credits from starting my degree in Scotland, but realizing I needed math, science, and sunshine in my life was worth the time and tuition. Landing at Pitt has enabled me to take advantage of a school that has everything I need to set myself up for success.
